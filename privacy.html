Privacy Policy

Whereabouts · last updated 2026

Whereabouts is a private family calendar for planning where each person and pet is intended to be. This policy explains what it stores and how it is handled.

What it stores

The information you enter - family members, places, and placements (where someone is on a given day) - plus your display preferences such as colors. That's it. Whereabouts is not an event calendar and does not track anyone's real-time or GPS location.

Where it's stored

Data is kept in your own account's private database and cached on your device so the app works offline. It syncs across the devices signed in to your family account. It is not shared with other users or sold to anyone.

Accounts

Signing in uses an email and password to authenticate your family account. We store your email for sign-in only. We don't use it for advertising.

Third parties

The app loads standard web libraries and fonts from public content-delivery networks to run in your browser. It does not include advertising or third-party analytics trackers.

Your control

You can edit or delete your family, places, and placements at any time inside the app, and sign out to clear the local copy on a device. To have your account data removed entirely, contact us.

Children

Whereabouts is a tool for a household to organize itself. Entries about children are created and controlled by the family account holder, not by the children.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Reach out to the family account owner who set up this app.
